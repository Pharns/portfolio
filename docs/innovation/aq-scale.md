*Patent-Pending · Governance Maturity Measurement Standard · Filed April 25, 2026 · U.S. Provisional 64/049,300*

!!! success "For hiring managers — AI governance / measurement standards"

    **What I built:** A measurement standard for AI agent governance maturity — the AQ Scale™ — that produces a fixed-range governance maturity score from a structured assessment.

    **What it answers:** *"How governed are the AI agents in this organization?"* — the question every CISO, auditor, and procurement reviewer is now being asked, and almost no one can answer with a number.

    **Why this matters:** AI agent governance has the same problem credit risk had before FICO — everyone agreed it mattered, no one agreed on how to measure it. AQ Scale™ is the measurement layer.

---

## The measurement problem

AI agent governance has rapidly become a compliance, procurement, and board-level concern. EU AI Act enforcement begins August 2, 2026. NIST AI RMF and ISO/IEC 42001 are entering procurement contracts. Insurance carriers are starting to underwrite AI risk.

But there is no standard answer to a simple question: **how do you measure whether an organization actually governs its AI agents?**

Without a measurement standard, every assessment is bespoke, every comparison is apples-to-oranges, and every procurement conversation re-litigates the definition of "governed."

AQ Scale™ is the standard that closes that gap.

---

## What AQ Scale™ is

AQ Scale™ is a **governance maturity measurement standard** for AI agent operations. It produces a fixed-range score from a structured assessment of how an organization governs its AI agents at runtime.

- **Standardized.** Every score is computed the same way, against the same criteria.
- **Comparable.** Two organizations can be compared on the same scale.
- **Auditable.** The score derives from a structured assessment, not an opinion.
- **Patent-pending.** The scoring methodology is covered under U.S. Provisional Patent Application 64/049,300.
